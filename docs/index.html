How the Slot Engine Works

What is this?

A Monte Carlo Simulation Engine for slot game math design. It models a full 3×5 reel game with 20-payline win evaluation, cascade tumble mechanic, scatter-triggered free spins, and Dragon Link–style Hold & Spin with a four-tier jackpot system. Run millions of simulated spins in seconds to compute theoretical RTP, volatility, hit rates, and win distributions — the same analysis used by commercial casino math teams.

The simulation runs in a Web Worker so your browser stays responsive during long runs. A Python engine (see GitHub) handles deeper analysis: multiprocessing across CPU cores, Welford online variance, and a binary-search RTP tuner.

📊 Key Metrics

  • RTP (Return to Player)
    Percentage of wagered money returned over millions of spins. 95% RTP = house keeps 5%. Decomposed into Base / Bonus / Hold & Spin contributions.
  • Volatility Index
    Standard deviation of win-per-spin ÷ bet. High volatility = rare but massive wins. Low = frequent small wins.
  • Hit Rate
    Fraction of spins returning any payout > 0.
  • Win Distribution
    Buckets on log scale: 0×, 0–1×, 1–5×, 5–15×, 15–50×, 50×+. Shows the shape of the payout curve.

🎰 Game Mechanics

  • Base Game + Cascades
    3×5 grid evaluated across 20 paylines left-to-right with wild substitution. Winning symbols are removed and new ones drop in (tumble/cascade), chaining wins from a single bet.
  • Free Spins
    Triggered by 3+ Scatter (SC) symbols anywhere. Awards 10 free spins at 2× multiplier with retrigger cap (100 max). H&S does not fire inside free spins.
  • Hold & Spin
    Triggered by 6+ Coin (CO) symbols. Coins lock; 3 lives reset on each new coin. Full screen = Grand Jackpot added to coin total. Mini / Minor / Major jackpots from weighted pool.
  • Feature Buy (100× Bet)
    Directly purchases a guaranteed Hold & Spin session with 6 coins pre-locked. Calibrated to ~95% RTP on the buy cost.
